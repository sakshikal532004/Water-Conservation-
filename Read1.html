Top Tips to Save Water

1. Showering Habits

  • Shorten Shower Time:
    • Aim for 3-5 minute showers.
    • Use a timer or a 4-minute song.
    • Example: WaterAid's 4-minute shower song playlist.
  • Shower vs. Bath:
    • Prefer showers over baths to conserve water.
    • If taking a bath, consider sharing.
  • During Shower:
    • Turn off water while applying soap, shampoo, or conditioner.
    • Descale shower head to maintain water pressure.
  • Use Water Saving Devices:
    • Install regulated or eco shower heads.

2. Tap Usage

  • Turn off Tap While Brushing Teeth:
    • Only use water for rinsing.
  • Fix Leaky Taps:
    • Maintain appliances to prevent water waste.

3. Water Reuse

  • Wash Vegetables in a Bowl:
    • Reuse the water for flushing toilets or watering plants.
  • Reuse Washing-Up Water:
    • Use it to water plants.
  • Reuse Bath Water:
    • Use it to wash patios.
  • Collect Cold Water:
    • When waiting for hot water, collect the cold water for toilet flushing.
  • Reuse Vegetable Boiling Water:
    • Use it for making gravy.
  • YouTube Videos:
    • Water Conservation

4. Rainwater Collection

  • Install Water Butts:
    • Collect rainwater for garden use.
    • Add a rainwater diverter to guttering downpipe.
    • Water from rainwater filled water butts can be used during hosepipe bans.

5. Kettle Usage

  • Boil Only Necessary Water:
    • Measure water with your cup before filling the kettle.
    • Use kettles with a "one cup" function.

6. Appliance Usage

  • Run Full Loads:
    • Only operate washing machines and dishwashers when fully loaded.
  • Maintain Appliances:
    • Fix running toilets.

7. Other Water Saving Devices

  • Hot water dispensers for single cups of tea.
